🎮 Gaming Industry

Gaming M&A Hits $100B+ in Q1 2026 — A 15-Month High

Q1 2026 saw 51 M&A transactions in gaming with over $100 billion in disclosed deal value, the highest in 15 months, according to Drake Star's Global Gaming Report. The quarter was headlined by Paramount's $110B acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery (including its games division) and Savvy Games Group's $6B acquisition of mobile giant Moonton.

💡 Why it matters: The gaming M&A wave is accelerating exactly as GTA 6 (expected November 2026) could catalyze a new bull cycle in gaming equities. Mid-market esports and creator-gaming companies operating in this space — like GameSquare — sit in the sweet spot of strategic acquirer interest.

XR Hardware Dominates Private Financing; AI + UGC Lead Investment Themes

🤖 AI in Marketing

74% of Marketers Now Use AI in Influencer Campaigns — But Avoid Virtual Influencers

Linqia's 2026 State of Influencer Marketing report (200+ enterprise marketers surveyed) found that 74% use AI to generate ideas, write briefs, and streamline workflows. Yet 89% still avoid virtual influencers, and 79% say ROI measurement remains their biggest challenge. 62% are increasing influencer budgets in 2026, with 33% planning to spend over $5M.

💡 Why it matters: AI is being adopted as workflow tooling, not as a creative replacement — which means the human creator relationship remains central. Platforms that embed AI into campaign ops while keeping creator authenticity intact will own the market. Attribution and ROI measurement is still an enormous, unsolved gap.

💡 Why it matters: $43.9B is not a niche channel — it's core marketing infrastructure. For companies like GameSquare operating at the gaming/creator intersection in the US market, this is a massive addressable market growing at an extraordinary rate with institutional budget behind it.

🇪🇺 Spain & EU Regulation

EU Signs "One Europe, One Market" Roadmap — Digital Platform Rules Tighten

On April 24, the Presidents of the European Parliament, Council, and Commission signed a Joint Declaration committing to the "One Europe, One Market" roadmap — an integration push that accelerates harmonized digital market rules across the bloc. This follows the ongoing DSA (Digital Services Act) enforcement track and the DMA (Digital Markets Act) designations of major platforms as "gatekeepers."

💡 Why it matters: EU platform regulation is entering its enforcement phase in 2026. For creator economy companies operating in Spain and Europe, DSA compliance requirements around algorithmic transparency, content moderation, and advertising targeting are becoming operational realities — and compliance costs are real. Companies that get ahead of this now will have structural advantage in European markets.
