🎮 Gaming Industry

Gaming M&A Hits 15-Month High in Q1 2026: 51 Deals, $100B+ in Value

Global gaming dealmaking hit a 15-month high in Q1 2026 with 51 M&A transactions and over $100 billion in disclosed value, per Drake Star's Global Gaming Report. The structural consolidation in gaming mirrors what's happening in creator-adjacent media. The XR hardware surge signals the next platform wave. GTA 6 (projected November 2026) remains the sector's single biggest potential sentiment catalyst for H2.

💼 M&A

Creator Economy Roll-Ups Confirmed: Fixated Acquires Studio71 in 5th Deal in 5 Months

LA-based Fixated acquired Studio71 — a creator network generating ~$290M in revenue in FY2025 — from ProSiebenSat.1. The deal closed Monday. It's Fixated's fifth acquisition in approximately five months (after Camp Talent, Moondust Management, Ellify, and Elevate), backed by a $50M strategic investment from Eldridge Industries. The combined entity now manages 1,000+ creators with billions of monthly views across YouTube, TikTok, Snap, Twitch, and more. Forbes calls this "the clearest signal yet that the creator economy has entered a full consolidation cycle."

The roll-up playbook PE has used in every other fragmented industry for three decades has landed in creator media. Quartermast's 2026 M&A forecast says infrastructure businesses are the prized targets — exactly where creator network and distribution platforms sit. The window for acqui-hires and bolt-on deals at reasonable multiples is compressing fast.

🇺🇸 US Market

GameSquare Executes Largest Monthly Buyback, Expands Repurchase Program to $15M

GameSquare Holdings (NASDAQ: GAME) executed its largest monthly share repurchase to date in April 2026 — buying back ~2.3 million shares at an average of $0.44/share (~$1M total). On April 14, the Board expanded the buyback program from $5M to $15M, citing "accelerating profitability, strong balance sheet, and confidence in long-term earnings power." The stock trades at $0.55 as of late April — management is buying meaningfully below market.

A tripled buyback authorization combined with the largest-ever monthly repurchase is a strong capital allocation signal from GAME management. At $0.44 average acquisition cost vs. $0.55 market price, this implies book value conviction — worth tracking closely as the creator economy M&A cycle heats up around them.

🇪🇺 Spain / EU Regulation

DSA Second Wave: Snapchat Under Formal Investigation, Mid-Tier Platforms Now in Scope

The EU's DSA enforcement is entering a second, more aggressive wave. On March 26, the European Commission opened a formal investigation into Snapchat (illicit sales, minor safeguarding failures) and issued non-compliance findings against four major adult platforms. Separately, a January 2026 investigation into X focused on AI tool Grok's risk assessment failures. Analysis from HowSociable (April 2026) shows the Commission is now expanding DSA obligations to mid-tier platforms — not just VLOPs — in what regulators describe as a "systematic risk pipeline."

For any platform or creator-economy company operating in the EU with 45M+ monthly users in range, DSA compliance is no longer a distant concern — it's a live commercial risk. Spain-based operations serving EU audiences need documented content moderation and transparency infrastructure now.
