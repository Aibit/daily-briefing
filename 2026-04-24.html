🤖 AI in Marketing

AI Accelerates Creator Output — But Fraud Rates Hit 15–30% of Influencer Spend

AI is dramatically lowering the barrier to content creation in 2026, but disproportionately benefits established creators with existing audiences — widening the gap between top-tier and new entrants. Meanwhile, influencer fraud rates have reached an estimated 15–30% of total influencer marketing spend depending on platform and vertical. FTC enforcement has intensified sharply, with over 60 formal actions against creators and brands in the past 18 months, and the agency has signalled acceleration.

Fraud and compliance risk are becoming a meaningful factor in influencer deal structures. Platforms and tools that solve for verified attribution and disclosure automation are gaining pricing power.

🇪🇺 Spain / EU Regulation

EU Disclosure & AI Influencer Rules: Spain at the Frontier, Enforcement Looming

With the DSA now fully in effect for all platforms and the EU AI Act phasing in through 2026, AI-generated virtual influencers like Aitana López (The Clueless, Barcelona) are squarely in the crosshairs. The EU's draft guidelines on synthetic media disclosure would require platforms to label AI-generated commercial content. At the same time, Spain's CNMC has signalled closer oversight of influencer commercial disclosures, mirroring the FTC's aggressive enforcement posture in the US (60+ actions in 18 months).

Any platform or agency operating in Spain with AI talent or influencer marketing components needs a compliance strategy now — the enforcement window is closing fast. This is a risk factor and a moat opportunity for compliant players.
