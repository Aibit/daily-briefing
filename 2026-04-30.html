🇪🇺 Spain / EU Regulation

DSA Enforcement Is the New Platform Shock: Snapchat & X Under Formal EU Investigation

On March 26, the European Commission escalated DSA enforcement by launching a formal investigation into Snapchat (illicit sales, grooming, minors protection) and issuing findings against four major adult-content platforms for inadequate safeguards. In January, the Commission also launched a formal DSA investigation into X, specifically targeting Grok AI's risks within the platform under arts. 34, 35, and 42.

The EU has now built a structured enforcement pipeline: investigation → enforcement → outcome. Platforms with 45M+ EU monthly users (VLOPs) must moderate content, audit risks, and maintain ad transparency — or face fines up to 6% of global revenue.

💡 Eduard: DSA compliance is becoming a systemic operational cost for any platform playing in the EU. For creator economy businesses with EU ambitions (hatchet.cool, GameSquare), understanding DSA obligations early is a competitive moat — not a compliance checkbox.
Source: Atlas Institute · EU Crim (DSA overview)
